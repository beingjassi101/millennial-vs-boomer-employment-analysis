Key Findings

What the Data Tells Us

01

The Experience Premium

Baby Boomers captured nearly all net employment growth post-recession — +1.95M vs +110K for Millennials. Their established tenure protected them from layoffs and positioned them for gains in management and skilled roles.

02

Structural Blue-Collar Displacement

Millennials lost 478K construction and 317K production jobs — sectors with disproportionately high Millennial participation pre-2008. This signals lasting structural displacement, not a temporary cycle.

03

Healthcare Is Recession-Proof

Registered Nurses, Home Health Aides, and Personal Care Aides topped growth lists for both generations. Healthcare is the one sector where demand is fundamentally independent of economic cycles.

04

Energy Drove Millennial Recovery

The only robust Millennial job markets were in energy-producing states (North Dakota, Texas, Wyoming, Alaska). Without the oil boom, Millennial employment decline would have been far more severe nationally.
